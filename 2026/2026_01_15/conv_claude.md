### Clip Length & Capabilities

Current state-of-the-art models like Google Veo 3 and Runway Gen-4.5 generate video clips around 8 seconds long, though some models like Sora 2 can produce clips up to 25 seconds in storyboard mode with claims of 120-second base generations. ### Costs (Compute & Dollars)

Current pricing varies significantly by model and quality tier:

- Google Veo 3.1 Fast costs $0.15 per second, Standard runs $0.40 per second, and the premium Full tier charges $0.75 per second
- Veo 2 costs 50 cents per second, which adds up to $30 per minute or $1,800 per hour
- A $20/month plan on tools like Seedance delivers around 25 five-second HD clips

For a feature-length 90-minute film at current rates, you'd need ~5,400 seconds of video, costing approximately **$810-$4,050** depending on the quality tier selected.

### Character Consistency

This is currently one of the biggest challenges. Character consistency requires manual work through techniques like "frame-to-frame chaining" where creators grab the last frame of each finished segment and upload it as reference for the next prompt.

Veo 3.1's "Ingredients to Video" feature allows users to add up to three reference images of characters to achieve consistency between shots. However, the character's voice currently shifts from one clip to the next, even when generated by the same AI video model.

**Current status**: Possible but labor-intensive. Visual consistency is achievable with proper workflows, but voice consistency across clips remains unsolved.
